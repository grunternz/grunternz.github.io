Entire year, 2014

Software Engineering Group Project

A self-oriented analysis of my contributions to the group project.

Goal

As part of a team of six, we were tasked with creating a Java application over the course of two semesters that would aid writers and the like in their creative process.

Process

Thrown into the deep end from the start, we were expected to use an agile development cycle spanning six sprints of varying lengths throughout the course of two semesters; breaks included. Assigned with a pre-determined list of features and requirements that our product owner reguarly groomed and adjusted according to his tastes, desires and our feedback as developers. Grooming usually occurred before each sprint planning meeting however sometimes it was conducted in conjunction with the planning meetings, especially earlier on as we were learning. This allowed for a greater level of communication as both parties could weigh in at the same time. Every team member was expected to and held accountable for keeping track of their time spent on various stories and tasks. We made use of the open source Agilefant platform to not only document our time spent, but also break down stories into smaller more specific and manageable tasks while also tracking our overall progression. Stand-up meetings were expected of us and as such a scrum master was to be a stand-in on half of the required meetings per week, providing their advice while also helping remove any hinderances, and diffusing tension within the group.

Contributions

Sprint One

In our initial sprint we worked together to get the core framework of the application up and running before splitting off into smaller sub-groups to complete various tasks. My focus was getting rudimentary undo redo functionality working as it was a functional requirement for the project. In order to achieve this I made use of the memento pattern wherein the state of the application is dereferenced, serialised and stored on a stack whenever an undoable action occurs. The stack is then methodically manipulated to achieve the desired undo redo functionality.

The module I wrote consisted of three main component parts;

  • Caretaker: Manages a collection of previous Memento versions.
  • Originator: Creates, assigns to, gets and sets Mementos.
  • Memento: Object to be stored in difference states.

Sprint Two

Junit tests for undo redo, UX: accelerators and mnemonics, looking into fest. throw low catch high, orthogonal tests, confined testing scope.

Sprint Three

Comparators for list display, web look and feel, bug fixing

Sprint Four

Recycle Bin, Status bar

Sprint Five

JGraphX, getting the gui functionality working, implementing the sandbox, writing layout managers, gui buttons, perspectives

Sprint Six

Major bug fixing, finding long known elusive bugs, gui icon overhaul
